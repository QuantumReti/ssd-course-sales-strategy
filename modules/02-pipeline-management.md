# Module 02: Pipeline Management

## Why Pipeline Management Is Non-Negotiable

A sales pipeline without discipline is just a wishlist. Every lead that goes un-followed-up, every conversation that falls through the cracks, every proposal that sits without a response - these are real revenue losses.

Mason's job is to ensure that every qualified lead is tracked, every follow-up is timely, and no deal dies of neglect.

---

## The 5-Stage Pipeline

### Stage 1: New Lead
Definition: First contact made or received. Conversation may not have started yet.

What happens here:
- Lead is logged with source (Google Maps cold outreach, Facebook group, referral, inbound website, WhatsApp)
- Basic info captured: business name, owner name, contact number, industry, suburb
- Initial message sent (warm or cold open)

Time target: Move from New Lead to Qualified within 48 hours of first contact

### Stage 2: Qualified
Definition: BANT assessment complete. Budget ready or nearly ready, authority confirmed, need identified (score 5+), timeline within 30 days.

What happens here:
- Need score recorded (1-10)
- Decision-maker confirmed
- Timeline noted
- Pain points documented
- Ready for solution presentation

Time target: Present solution within 24-48 hours of qualification

### Stage 3: Proposal Sent
Definition: Solution presented. Price discussed. Follow-up sequence activated.

What happens here:
- WhatsApp summary sent (Day 0 follow-up)
- Payment link or booking link sent
- Follow-up sequence begins (see Sales Fundamentals Module 07)
- Objections noted and addressed

Time target: Close or escalate within 7 days

### Stage 4: Negotiating
Definition: Active back-and-forth. They have not said yes but they have not said no. Real engagement happening.

What happens here:
- Objection handling in real-time
- Partner or Josh involvement considered for complex deals
- Modified proposal (different tier, different entry point) if needed
- Clear deadline set for decision

Time target: Resolution within 5 days (yes, no, or escalate to Josh)

### Stage 5: Closed Won / Closed Lost
Closed Won: Payment received, onboarding triggered, client file created.
Closed Lost: Lead tagged with loss reason, moved to nurture sequence or archived.

## Daily Pipeline Review

Every day, Mason should review the pipeline and answer these questions:

1. What moved forward yesterday? (log progress)
2. What is stuck? (identify reason - objection? silence? timing?)
3. What needs a follow-up today? (action it)
4. What has been sitting in the same stage for 7+ days? (escalate, adjust, or close as lost)
5. What new prospects can be added today? (keep the top of funnel full)

Time required: 10-15 minutes per day. Non-negotiable.

## Pipeline Velocity: Average Days in Each Stage

| Stage | Target Days | Action If Stuck |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| New Lead to Qualified | 1-2 days | Follow up harder, try different channel |
| Qualified to Proposal | 1-2 days | Send solution presentation immediately |
| Proposal to Close | 3-7 days | Activate full follow-up sequence |
| Negotiating to Resolution | 3-5 days | Involve Josh if needed |

Total pipeline velocity target: New Lead to Closed Won in under 14 days.

Deals that go past 21 days without closing almost never close. Energy is better invested elsewhere.

## Lead Scoring

Every lead gets a score from 1-10 based on four factors. This determines how much time and energy to invest.

### Scoring Matrix

| Factor | Low (1-3) | Medium (4-6) | High (7-10) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Urgency | No rush, exploring | Some pain, 1-2 months | Active pain, ready now |
| Budget | Not sure / low budget | Budget probably there | Ready to invest |
| Authority | Not the decision-maker | One of two decision-makers | Sole decision-maker |
| Fit | Marginal fit | Good fit | Perfect ICP match |

Total score: Add all four. 
- 28-40: Priority 1 - drop everything and focus here
- 16-27: Priority 2 - active follow-up, maintain sequence
- Under 16: Priority 3 - nurture only, low time investment

### Scoring in Practice

When finishing a qualifying call, immediately score the lead and tag it in the CRM.

Example:
- Plumber in Aspley, sole owner, wants to be found on Google, ready now, just had a competitor take 3 jobs from him this week
- Urgency: 9 (active pain right now)
- Budget: 8 (owner-operated, expressed willingness)
- Authority: 10 (sole decision-maker)
- Fit: 9 (perfect ICP)
- Total: 36/40 - Drop everything. Present today. Close today.

## CRM Hygiene Rules

A clean CRM is a productive CRM. Every logged interaction should enable the next person who looks at this record to understand exactly where the lead is and what happens next.

### What Every Lead Record Must Have
- Business name and owner name
- Contact method (WhatsApp number, email, phone)
- Source (how we found them)
- Industry and suburb
- Lead score
- Current pipeline stage
- Last interaction date and summary
- Next action and scheduled date

### Non-Negotiable Logging Rules
1. Every conversation logged within 2 hours of ending
2. Every follow-up attempt noted (sent, no response, awaiting reply)
3. Every objection noted with the response used
4. Stage updated immediately when lead moves
5. No leads in limbo - every lead has a next action date

### Limbo = Money Left on the Table
A lead with no next action date is a lead that will be forgotten. If the next step is follow up in 7 days, set a reminder for 7 days. If it is wait and see, that is not acceptable - define what you are waiting to see and when you will take action.

## Win/Loss Analysis

Every closed deal (won or lost) should be reviewed. This is how we get better.

### Questions for Closed Won
- What was the original source?
- What was the lead score?
- What objections came up and how were they resolved?
- How many touches before close?
- What was the deciding factor?

### Questions for Closed Lost
- At what stage did we lose them?
- What was their stated reason?
- What was the real reason? (not always the same)
- Was there anything we could have done differently?
- Should we re-engage in 30-60 days?

### Monthly Win/Loss Review Format
- Total closes: [X]
- Close rate from proposals: [X]%
- Top 3 reasons for wins: [list]
- Top 3 reasons for losses: [list]
- One thing to improve next month: [specific change]
