# Module 01: Target Market Strategy

## Primary ICP - Ideal Customer Profile

The Sun State Digital ideal customer profile is specific and intentional. Trying to serve everyone means serving no one well.

### Primary ICP Definition
- Location: Brisbane and South-East Queensland (SEQ) - within commuting or service radius
- Business type: Service-based, owner-operated
- Employee count: 1-10 (most commonly 1-4)
- Annual revenue: 200K-2M (enough to pay for digital, not so big they need enterprise solutions)
- Current digital presence: None, weak, or outdated (the opportunity)
- Decision-maker: The owner is on WhatsApp or Facebook and makes the call themselves
- Mindset: Wants results, not jargon. Will act if the value is clear and the risk is low.

## Top 10 Verticals by Opportunity

These are ranked by a combination of market size, digital marketing adoption (low = higher opportunity), and average customer lifetime value.

### 1. Plumbing
- Market: Hundreds of licensed plumbers across Brisbane, most with weak or no web presence
- Pain: Emergency plumbing is high-intent, high-value, and almost entirely search-driven
- Opportunity: A plumber ranking for emergency plumber [suburb] gets calls worth hundreds per job
- Average deal value: High (website + SEO + care plan = strong LTV)

### 2. Electrical
- Market: Similar to plumbing in size and digital maturity
- Pain: Safety and urgency drive search - people do not ask a friend, they Google immediately
- Opportunity: Solar-adjacent electricians are growing fast and need strong digital presence

### 3. HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)
- Market: Brisbane summers create massive seasonal demand
- Pain: Installation and repair searches spike November-February and June-August
- Opportunity: Seasonal timing creates urgency - easy close before summer

### 4. Landscaping
- Market: Large suburban homeowner population in Brisbane's outer suburbs
- Pain: Before and after content is powerful - very visual industry
- Opportunity: Spring seasonal timing. Strong social proof through project photos.

### 5. Dental
- Market: Private dental clinics competing heavily for new patients
- Pain: New patient acquisition is expensive through traditional channels
- Opportunity: Google reviews and local SEO are the #1 factor in new patient decisions
- Note: Higher budget awareness - more likely to invest in full packages

### 6. Physiotherapy and Allied Health
- Market: Growing private practice market in Brisbane
- Pain: Patients choose based on reviews, proximity, and website credibility
- Opportunity: Many practices have outdated websites and zero review strategy
- Note: Often have practice managers who can advocate internally

### 7. Cafe and Restaurant
- Market: Thousands of venues in Brisbane - very competitive
- Pain: Google Maps and review presence drives walk-in and delivery decisions
- Opportunity: GBP optimisation and social media have disproportionate impact on foot traffic
- Note: Hospitality is price-sensitive - lead with GBP and social before upselling full website

### 8. Legal (Lawyers and Solicitors)
- Market: Competitive, but many suburban and family law practices are digitally weak
- Pain: People Google lawyers the same way they Google plumbers - high intent, trust-driven
- Opportunity: A family law firm ranking for family lawyer [suburb] captures high-value clients
- Note: Higher expectations around website quality and professionalism

### 9. Cleaning (Residential and Commercial)
- Market: Large market, many sole operators and small teams
- Pain: Highly competitive on price - differentiation through reviews and online presence is key
- Opportunity: Regular recurring service = predictable revenue for the cleaning business = they value reliability

### 10. Building and Construction
- Market: Renovations and new builds - Brisbane building market is large and active
- Pain: Project photos are the primary sales tool but most builders do not have a website to show them on
- Opportunity: Before and after projects on a fast website with SEO = significant lead generation

## Secondary Markets

After Brisbane/SEQ is established, expand to:

### Sunshine Coast
- 3rd fastest growing region in Australia
- Strong tradie market (construction, landscaping, HVAC)
- Less competition than Brisbane for most trades

### Gold Coast
- Larger population than Sunshine Coast
- Tourism-adjacent hospitality and beauty businesses
- Highly competitive tradies market - need strong SEO differentiation

### Toowoomba
- Largest inland city in Queensland
- Agricultural and trade businesses dominate
- Far less digital competition than southeast corridor

### Ipswich
- Fast-growing western corridor
- Young homeowners = tradies in high demand
- Underserved digital market

## Exclusions - Who We Do NOT Target

### eCommerce Businesses
- Need product photography, inventory management, shipping integrations, and ongoing content at scale
- Not our core offering - refer out

### B2B SaaS and Tech Companies
- Different buyer journey (long sales cycle, multiple stakeholders, product demos)
- Not a fit for our service model

### National Chains and Franchises
- Decisions made centrally, not by local operators
- Marketing managed at corporate level
- Too complex and slow to close

### Real Estate Agencies
- Own vertical with specific compliance requirements
- Portal-dependent (REA Group, Domain) in ways that make standard websites less impactful
- May revisit as a separate vertical later

## Finding Targets

### Method 1: Google Maps + No Website Filter
Search [service] in [Brisbane suburb] on Google Maps. Click through to business profiles. Businesses with no website link or a broken website link are prime targets.

Process:
1. Search plumber in Chermside on Google Maps
2. Click each result
3. No website link = reach out immediately
4. Has a website = visit the site. Outdated (pre-2020 design, not mobile-friendly, no reviews) = still a prospect

### Method 2: Facebook Community Groups
Brisbane has hundreds of active local Facebook groups where business owners and residents interact.

Strategy:
- Join groups like Brisbane Business Owners, Brisbane North Tradies, and suburb-specific groups
- Monitor for posts asking for business recommendations
- When a recommendation request matches our verticals, contact the businesses being mentioned
- Never spam the group - engage authentically

### Method 3: Referrals from Existing Clients
Every new client is a referral source. At 30 days post-launch (when they are happy), ask:
Do you know anyone else who could use a website like yours? We give a month free to both of you if they sign up.

This is our highest-quality and lowest-cost acquisition channel.

### Method 4: LinkedIn Local Business Owners
Search for owners in specific industries and suburbs. LinkedIn is less saturated than other channels for this type of outreach in Brisbane.

Approach:
- Connect with a personalised note (mention a specific thing about their business)
- After connection, send the qualifying opening message
- Do not pitch immediately - build rapport first

## Prospecting Volume Targets

To hit 20 new clients per month, Mason needs to operate at scale:

| Activity | Daily Target | Monthly Total |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| New prospects identified | 20 | 400 |
| Meaningful conversations started | 5 | 100 |
| Qualified leads (BANT complete) | 2 | 40 |
| Proposals sent | 1 | 20 |
| Closes | 1 | 20 |

Conversion math:
- 400 prospects identified
- 100 conversations started (25% contact rate)
- 40 qualified (40% of conversations)
- 20 proposals sent (50% of qualified)
- 10 closes (50% close rate on proposals)

Note: These numbers improve significantly as social proof and brand recognition build. Month 6 will be much easier than Month 1.

## Vertical Messaging Matrix

Different verticals respond to different pain points. Adapt your opening message accordingly.

| Vertical | Primary Pain | Opening Hook |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Plumbers | Missing emergency calls | When someone Googles emergency plumber [suburb] right now, are you showing up? |
| Electricians | Invisible vs licensed competitors | Safety and trust are everything in electrical - your online presence needs to reflect that |
| Landscapers | No way to show project quality | Your work speaks for itself - but not if no one can find it online |
| Dentists | Patient acquisition cost | New patients Google you before they call. What do they find? |
| Cafes | Foot traffic and Google Maps | How many people are walking past because they checked Google and moved on? |
| Lawyers | Professional credibility | When someone searches family lawyer in [suburb], are you the one they trust? |
| Cleaners | Standing out in a crowded market | In cleaning, reviews and online presence separate the businesses that grow from those that stay still |
| Builders | Showing project quality | Your last renovation is someone else's dream - but only if they can find you online |
