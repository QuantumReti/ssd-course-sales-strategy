# Module 04: Referral and Retention Strategy

## Why Retention Is More Valuable Than Acquisition

Acquiring a new client costs 5-7 times more than retaining an existing one. A client who stays for 12 months is worth dramatically more than one who cancels after 2. And a retained, happy client is your most reliable source of referrals.

The formula: Deliver results + deliver service = retention + referrals = exponential growth.

## The Referral Strategy

### When to Ask for a Referral

Timing is everything. Ask too soon and it feels transactional. Ask at the right moment and it feels natural.

The ideal referral ask window: 30 days after launch, when they are happy and have seen early results.

Signs they are ready to be asked:
- They have mentioned a positive result (got a call from Google, review came in, someone commented on the website)
- They responded positively to the last check-in message
- They gave you a Google review unprompted

### The Referral Ask Script

Hey [Name]! Really glad the site is going well. Quick question - do you know any other local business owners who are in the same spot you were a few months ago? Trying to get online but not sure where to start?

We have a referral deal where if someone you send us signs up, you both get a month free. Zero effort on your part - just a text saying hey, check these guys out.

What works about this script:
- It is casual and warm, not a formal sales ask
- It reminds them of their own before state (creates empathy for the referral)
- The incentive is clear and simple
- It lowers the effort bar to almost zero (just a text)

### Referral Incentive Structure

The default referral deal: Both the referrer and the new client get 1 month of their service fee credited.

Example:
- Existing client pays 119 per month, refers a friend
- If friend signs up: existing client gets 119 credit (one month free), new client gets 119 credit
- Net cost to SSD: 238 credit value
- Value of the new client (assuming 12-month LTV at 119 per month): 1,428 in revenue
- Referral ROI: extremely positive

Note: Never give cash for referrals. Credits or service discounts are cleaner, more loyal, and avoid compliance complexity.

## Building a Professional Referral Network

Beyond client referrals, build strategic partnerships with other professionals who serve the same SMB market.

### Top Referral Partner Categories

Accountants and Bookkeepers:
- They see every small business's financials
- When a client is doing well and ready to invest, accountants know
- Pitch: We help your clients get found online and grow revenue - makes their numbers look better too
- Approach: LinkedIn outreach to Brisbane accounting firms

Business Coaches and Consultants:
- Work with small business owners on growth strategy
- A client who needs to grow needs marketing - they often refer to digital agencies
- Pitch: We handle the digital side so you can focus on the strategy
- Approach: Business networking groups (BNI Brisbane, local chambers of commerce)

Real Estate Agents (Commercial):
- When a business moves premises, they often need to update their online presence
- Perfect trigger for a new website
- Approach: Build relationships with commercial property agents in Brisbane

Web Designers and Freelancers:
- Freelancers often have clients who need ongoing SEO or care they cannot provide
- Refer the clients they cannot serve, they refer clients who outgrow DIY or freelance
- Approach: Facebook groups for Brisbane freelancers, web design communities

Financial Planners and Mortgage Brokers:
- Often advise business owners on investments and growth
- A growing client base = more income = more investable assets
- Pitch: Helping your clients grow their business helps you grow yours

## Retention: The Monthly Client Journey

### Day 1-7: Onboarding Experience
The first week after purchase sets the tone for the entire relationship.

What to deliver:
- Confirmation message immediately after payment
- Briefing call within 24 hours (or intake form)
- Website live within 24-48 hours of brief completion
- WhatsApp message when the site goes live: Your site is LIVE! Check it out: [link]. You are officially on Google.

What this creates: A wow moment. They have never had a website go live in 24 hours. They tell someone. That is a referral seed.

### Day 14: Soft Check-In
Hey [Name]! Just checking in - how is everything going with the new site? Any questions or changes you want to make?

This is not a sales call. It is a relationship call. Listen to any issues. Fix them fast.

### Day 30: Results Check-In + Referral Ask (If Results Are Good)
Review their Google Business Insights with them:
- How many people saw their profile
- How many clicked through to the website
- How many called directly from Google

Then make the referral ask (see above).

### Day 60: SEO Progress Update
Share ranking progress or Google Search Console data. Even if rankings are early stage, show them the trend.

This is also when the SEO upsell conversation is most natural if they are not already on the Growth plan.

### Day 90: Account Review
This is the most important check-in. Ask:
- How are things going overall?
- Are you happy with the results?
- Is there anything we could be doing better?
- What is your biggest business challenge right now?

This last question often opens the door to AI Jetpack, Google Ads, social media management, or other upsells - organically, based on their needs.

Also ask for the NPS score: On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend us to a mate?

## Net Promoter Score (NPS)

NPS is one of the most reliable indicators of client satisfaction and likely referral behaviour.

Ask at: 90 days post-launch (when they have seen results and have context to judge)

How to ask:
Hey [Name] - quick question. On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend Sun State Digital to another business owner? No wrong answers - just honest feedback.

Interpreting results:
- 9-10 (Promoters): Happy, loyal, likely to refer. Ask for a referral and a Google review.
- 7-8 (Passives): Satisfied but not enthusiastic. Find out what would make it a 9 or 10.
- 0-6 (Detractors): Unhappy. Intervention needed immediately. Call them, not text.

Anything under 8: Trigger an immediate conversation. Do not wait for the next scheduled check-in.

Ask: What would need to change for this to be a 9 or 10? Then do that thing.

## Churn Prevention

Churn = client cancels their subscription. Early detection is everything.

### Churn Warning Signs

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| No login activity for 30+ days | Check in proactively |
| Missed payment (card declined) | Contact immediately, do not let it lapse |
| Support ticket unresolved for 48+ hours | Escalate to Josh |
| Negative review or complaint on social | Respond publicly, resolve privately |
| No response to 2 consecutive check-ins | Phone call (not WhatsApp) |
| NPS score of 6 or below | Same-day callback |

### The Churn Prevention Call

When a warning sign appears:

Hey [Name], just wanted to give you a personal call - I noticed [specific thing] and wanted to make sure everything is going well on your end. Are there any concerns I can help sort out?

Then listen. Do not defend. Do not sell. Just listen and fix.

Most churn happens because of a small unresolved issue that was never raised. A proactive call prevents 80% of cancellations.

## Turning Churned Clients Into Referrers

Even when a client cancels, the relationship does not have to end badly.

The offboarding message:
Hey [Name] - we are really sorry to see you go. We hope we added some value while we worked together. If you ever want to come back, the door is always open - and our prices will still be the same.

One last thing: if you know anyone in business who could use a hand getting online, we would love to help them. Just send them our way.

Wish you all the best with the business!

Why this works: It is warm, no hard feelings, and it leaves the referral door open. Churned clients who had a good experience often refer - especially if the exit was handled graciously.
