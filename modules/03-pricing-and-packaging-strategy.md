# Module 03: Pricing and Packaging Strategy

## The Psychology of Pricing

How you present price matters as much as the price itself. The same number can feel like a bargain or an expense depending on context, framing, and sequence. Master the psychology of pricing and you stop competing on price entirely.

---

## Price Anchoring: Always Present High First

Price anchoring means establishing a high reference price before revealing your actual price. When the anchor is set first, your price looks dramatically more reasonable.

The anchor for Sun State Digital: Most agencies charge between 5,000 and 15,000 for a professional website. We do it for 199, live in 24 hours.

Without the anchor, 199 is just 199. With the anchor, 199 feels like a revelation.

Rules for anchoring:
1. Always anchor before revealing your price - never the reverse
2. The anchor must be believable and truthful (agency quotes in Brisbane genuinely are 5K+)
3. Do not anchor with your own higher package first - anchor against the market, then present your packages

## The 3-Tier Rule

Always offer 3 options. Never 1 (no choice = buyer's paralysis), never more than 3 (too many options = decision fatigue).

Why 3 works:
- The top option makes the middle option look reasonable
- The bottom option makes the middle option feel more premium
- Most people choose the middle option - so the middle is designed to win

## Discounting Rules

Discounts are not a sales strategy. They are a last resort and must always be exchanged for something of value.

### Never Discount Without Getting Something in Return

If a prospect asks for a discount, respond with:
I can look at what is possible - can I ask, is budget the main thing holding you back, or is there something else?

If they confirm it is purely budget:
What if we started with the Starter package at 199 and 20 per month - and I lock in the Growth package price for you when you are ready to add SEO? That way we get you live and earning now without the full commitment upfront.

### What You Can Exchange for a Discount

If you must discount (rare), always get one of these in return:
1. A referral commitment (introduce us to one business owner who needs a website)
2. A case study permission (we can use your business as an example and feature your results)
3. A longer commitment (pay 6 months upfront for a reduced rate)
4. A testimonial and Google review (guaranteed 5-star review within 7 days of launch)

### The Discount Danger

Every time you discount without reason, you:
- Signal that your prices are negotiable (they will try again)
- Undervalue the product in their mind (if it is cheap, it must be worth less)
- Set a bad precedent for the relationship

The better alternative to discounting is reframing value. Make the ROI undeniable, and price becomes irrelevant.

## Value-Based Pricing: Price the Outcome, Not the Deliverable

A website is not a deliverable. A flood of phone calls is an outcome.

Wrong framing: A professional website for 199.

Right framing: If this website brings you 2 extra jobs a month - at even 300 a job - that is 600 per month in new revenue for 119 per month in investment. That is a 5 to 1 return.

Value-based framing by vertical:

Plumber:
One emergency call from Google is worth 350-800 depending on the job. Our 119 per month package brings most plumbers 3-5+ extra calls per month. The ROI is obvious.

Dentist:
A new patient is worth 500-5,000+ in lifetime value. If our SEO work brings in 2 new patients per month, that is 1,000-10,000 in monthly value for 119-299 per month in investment.

Cafe:
Harder to attribute directly, but foot traffic from Google Maps and reviews is measurable through Google Business Insights (how many direction requests and calls per month).

The rule: Always present the outcome value before the cost. Make them do the math. Once they see the return, the price is just the cost of access.

## Upsell Sequencing

The best time to upsell is when the client is already happy and seeing results.

### The Upsell Timeline

Day 1 (at checkout):
Close website first. Then, before ending the conversation, add hosting and domain:
While I have you - let me grab the domain sorted now too. Is businessname.com.au available, or do you want to check? [Check together]. Perfect - I will add that in for 20 per month so everything is in one place.

Day 30 (after launch):
Client is live and starting to see results. SEO upsell:
Hey [Name]! Hope the site is going well. Just wanted to check in - are you getting any Google traffic yet? [Discuss results]. The next step to really accelerate this is the SEO package - for 99 a month we actively push you up the rankings. Most of our clients see movement in 60-90 days. Worth adding?

Day 60 (if on Growth package):
Consider Google Ads or social media upsell:
Hey [Name] - you have been on the Growth plan for 2 months now. Your SEO is moving nicely. Have you thought about adding Google Ads to get immediate traffic while the organic rankings build? Even 500 a month in ad spend can bring in 8-12 qualified leads. Happy to set it up for you.

Day 90 (check-in):
Full account review. Any service gaps? Any new business needs? Any referrals to capture?

The sequence is: Trust earns the first sale. Results earn the upsell. Service earns the referral.
